About Us

Established in 1760, Lal Bagh Botanical Garden is a historic garden featuring rare plants, ancient trees, and stunning glass houses. Spread over 240 acres, it serves as both a conservation center and popular tourist attraction.

The garden's iconic Glass House, inspired by London's Crystal Palace, hosts biannual flower shows attracting over 2 million visitors. Our 300-year-old Dodda Alada Mara (Big Banyan Tree) spreads across 3 acres, creating a natural cathedral of aerial roots.

As a center for botanical research, we maintain a seed bank preserving 450+ endangered species. Our Japanese garden, lotus pond, and prehistoric rock formations offer serene escapes within the bustling city.
